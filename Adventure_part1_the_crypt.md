# Adventure: The Ossuary Protocol
**Tone:** Grit, Dread, Low-Fantasy (Secretly a Simulation)
**Party:** 4 Players (Level 1)
**Setting:** Monotheistic (Faith of Mitra)
**Starting Equipment:** Players begin with **Improvised Weapons only** (1d4 damage, no bonuses). Proper weapons must be found or taken from enemies.

---

## PROLOGUE: THE GATEHOUSE TOWER
Before the players reach the monastery ruins, the path narrows to pass through the remnants of the monastery's outer gatehouse — a squat stone tower, half-collapsed, its arch still standing. A worn sun-disc carved above the arch marks it as Mitraic. This was once the formal entrance to the Broken Wing. The road through it is the only way forward.

### The Tower
Two bandits from Kaelen's crew have made camp here, pressed into the shelter of the remaining walls.

*   **Bandit Gregor** is asleep in a canvas hammock strung between two broken wall stubs, one arm hanging loose, his crossbow resting on the ground beneath him. He snores.
*   **Bandit Dav** is sitting on a chunk of fallen masonry near the arch, supposedly on watch — but he is absorbed in carving something into a stone with his knife, facing away from the approach path.

#### Approach Options
*   **DC 12 Stealth (group check):** The players can move through or around the tower without waking Gregor or alerting Dav. On a failure, Dav hears them, stands, and squints — not yet hostile: *"You lost? Camp's that way."*
*   **Conversation:** The bandits work for Kaelen and will direct the players toward the monastery camp. They assume anyone on this road is either lost or already in Kaelen's debt. They will not leave their post willingly.
*   **Combat:** Gregor wakes on his second turn (cannot act on round 1). Dav fights immediately. Neither will raise a general alarm if dealt with quickly — Kaelen doesn't expect reports from this post.

#### Loot
*   **Gregor's crossbow:** A serviceable light crossbow, **5 bolts** — he's been meaning to resupply. Likely the first proper ranged weapon the players will find. A **belt knife** hangs at his hip — well-worn, nothing special.
*   **Dav's blade:** A worn short sword — unusual for this crew, most of whom make do with clubs and knives. He's clearly had it longer than he's been working for Kaelen.
*   **The carving:** Dav has been scratching the outline of a woman's face into the stone. He has been at it a long time.

## I. ACT I: THE MONASTERY RUINS
The ruins of the Broken Wing monastery serve as a base for outcasts.

### The Bandit Camp
*   **Leader:** **Captain Kaelen "The Vulture"**. 
*   **The Conflict:** Kaelen holds a captured scout/player and demands the party retrieve a lead-lined reliquary from the crypts to buy their freedom.
*   **The Atmosphere:** Tense negotiation; bandits are wary of the "curse" on the mountain.

#### Layout
**The Cage Room** (small side chamber):
*   Two iron cages: one holds **a captured player**, the other a **wounded ranger** (an NPC who can become an ally if freed).
*   **1 Bandit guard** stands watch outside the cages — a heavy **club** resting against the wall beside him, a **belt knife** on his hip.
*   **1 Bandit** is inside the room, sitting near the door — half-asleep, bored. He has a **club** across his lap.
*   **1 Guard Dog** is chained near the cages — alert even when the bandits aren't.

**The Main Hall** (larger chamber, Kaelen's base):
*   **Kaelen's tent** is pitched in the centre of the room.
*   **Kaelen** is inside the tent along with **7 bandits** — resting, dicing, or sharpening blades. Kaelen wears a **patched hide armour** and carries a **longsword** at his hip — the only real sword in the camp that isn't borrowed or stolen off a corpse. Most of his crew are armed with **clubs and belt knives**; two of them carry **short swords**, worn openly like a status marker among the crew.

**The Monastery Exit:**
*   **2 Bandits** guard the way out of the monastery, posted at the main gate or archway leading outside. Both carry **clubs**; one has a **knife** tucked into his boot as well.

## II. ACT II: THE PILGRIM'S WAY
A treacherous ascent through steep cliffs and freezing rain.

### The Waystation
*   **The Scene:** A stone hut barred from within.
*   **Encounter:** One of the fallen priests on the ground stirs — his body convulses and he rises as a **Twitcher (Zombie)**.
*   **Loot:** **Warden’s Heavy Cloak** — a damp, lead-grey military cloak lined with oiled wool. Grants **Advantage on saving throws vs. weather-related exhaustion**; the dense weave acts as crude armour, granting **+1 AC** while worn (does not stack with armour that covers the torso). A deep interior pocket can conceal one small object (undetected on a DC 13 Perception check). *(Simulation hint: the hem never frays, no matter how rough the terrain.)*

### The Pilgrim's Shrine
A small niche cut into the cliff face on the final approach — a stone sun-disc, an offerings tray long since raided. Slumped against the base of the shrine is a **dead warden-priest**: an old man in a grey habit, stabbed once and left. He was the last keeper of the ossuary. The acolytes killed him here, took his notes, and then discovered they still couldn't open the wardrobe.
*   **The Body (no check to search):** A cord around his neck holds a **heavy iron key** — old, dark, the bow stamped with Mitra's sunburst. This opens the wardrobe in the Warden's Hut.
*   **DC 10 Investigation:** A folded note tucked inside the habit, written in a shaking hand: *"They know. I have burned what I could. The way below stays shut as long as the oak holds."*

## III. ACT III: THE FOUNDERS' OSSUARY
> **Full dungeon content in:** [`Adventure_part1_act3_the_ossuary.md`](Adventure_part1_act3_the_ossuary.md)

The Warden's Hut at the cliff face conceals the entrance to the ossuary — hidden behind a massive locked oak wardrobe built flush into the cliff wall. Stone steps descend into the sealed crypt below, where the **Cult of the True Script** has been operating for weeks. The dungeon ends with a confrontation with **Father Malachi** and the **Blood-Lock** ritual.

## V. LOOT & SIMULATION GLITCHES

### Consumables
*   **Vial of Bitters:** Healing Potion (2d4+2).
*   **Frame-Rate Tonic:** +2 AC and Advantage on Dex saves for 1 min.
*   **Whetstone of the Faithful:** +1 damage with one bladed weapon until next long rest (one use).
*   **Null-Silver Vial:** +2 CA for 1 hour.

### Simulation Hints (For the DM)
*   **The Sound:** The unsealed Amulet emits a high-pitched "fan noise" or white noise.
*   **The Visuals:** Rain patterns repeat perfectly; cliff edges look like "unloaded gray space".
*   **The Physics:** The stone altar feels like featureless, matte-gray plastic under the grime.
