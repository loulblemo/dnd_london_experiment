# Adventure: The Iron-Bleed Mine
**Tone:** Grit, Claustrophobia, Survival
**Party:** 4 Players (Level 1)
**Setting:** Goblin Hive, Dark Mine
**Key Mechanic:** The main shafts and connecting tunnels are raked by constant underground wind. Torches are extinguished within seconds in the passages. Some rooms sit in sheltered pockets deep in the rock — the wind doesn't reach them, and the goblins have crude torches burning there. But the tunnels between rooms are dark, and the guard post before the throne is deliberately kept lightless. The only reliable way to navigate the mine and find the exit is the **Grit-Snuff** — the black paste every goblin applies to their eyes to see in the dark.

---

## I. THE DESCENT

The entrance is a ragged shaft cut into the ridge face, propped open with timbers gone spongy with rot. A cold, iron-tasting wind flows out of it constantly — not a gust, but a steady, purposeful exhalation, as if the mountain is breathing out. Players who light a torch before entering will notice it gutter and lean sharply away from the entrance. It stays lit — barely — as long as they stand still in the open air.

The moment they step inside, the wind doubles. A torch will last roughly **30 seconds (3 rounds of movement)** before being snuffed completely.

**What the players notice before they enter:**
*   The timber framing around the entrance is smeared with greasy black marks at hand-height — not soot, but something deliberate, applied by many small hands over time.
*   **DC 10 Investigation:** The black marks have a faint chemical smell — acrid, like burnt pepper and mineral dust.
*   Just inside the entrance, propped against the wall as if left mid-shift, is a dead goblin — a few days old at least. Its eyes are ringed with thick black paste, dried to a crust. Whatever killed it, no one came to move it.

---

## II. THE MINE: A LIGHTLESS LABYRINTH

The Iron-Bleed Mine is not a simple tunnel. It is a network of branching shafts, collapsed side-passages, and low crawl-spaces that the goblins have been expanding haphazardly for months. The wind that pours through the main passages is strong enough to snuff a torch in seconds. But the mine is not uniformly dark — some chambers sit in sheltered pockets, cut into the rock at angles the airflow doesn't reach. In those rooms, the goblins have torches burning in iron brackets, and life proceeds in a dim, grimy light.

The goblins have solved the tunnel problem a different way. **Every goblin in the mine has their eyes rimmed with black paste — Grit-Snuff** — a compound ground from iron-rich ore, dried mushroom spore, and something chemical. It grants darkvision. They apply it everywhere, even in torchlit rooms, because the tunnels between rooms are always dark and they move through them constantly. The players will notice this in every room they enter: every goblin has black-rimmed, dead-looking eyes that do not flinch from light.

## III. THE ROOMS

### Room 1 — The Threshold Pit

*A wide entry chamber, the ceiling low and close. The floor is uneven packed earth scattered with rusted tools, broken pick handles, and the desiccated remains of a mule still in its harness. A large ore-cart sits derailed in the corner, tilted against the wall. The wind pours in from three different tunnel mouths in the far wall.*

**Enemies:** None.

**The Dark:** Even this close to the entrance, the wind is enough to snuff a torch in one round of movement.

**Details & Clues:**
*   **Three tunnel mouths** gape in the far wall. Two are false — one ends in a collapse after twenty feet, the other loops back to this room. The correct passage is the middle one. **DC 14 Perception** (with light) or **DC 10** (with darkvision): the middle tunnel floor is more worn — more foot traffic.
*   **The ore-cart:** Tipped on its side. Inside, half-buried under loose rubble, is a **cracked clay jar** still holding a finger-width of black paste. It has been opened recently. It smells acrid, like burnt pepper.
    *   **DC 12 Medicine or Nature:** This is a topical compound. Applying it to the eyes might enhance vision in the dark.
*   **The mule:** Its eyes are still open. The sockets are rimmed with the same black paste — smeared on post-mortem. An experiment. Or a habit.

**Foreshadowing:** The correct tunnel lets out a louder, steadier moan of wind as the players approach. Beyond it, in the dark, they can hear quiet clicking sounds — goblins communicating in the black.

---

### Room 2 — The Sifting Screen

*A wider chamber where ore was once sorted. The floor is gritty with black mineral dust. Rusted iron screens hang from ceiling brackets, some collapsed into piles of corroded mesh. The wind screams through the screen-holes and the gaps in the timber walls.*

**Enemies:** 3 Goblins.

**The Dark:** Any torch is extinguished the moment players enter. The screens funnel and accelerate the wind to a howl.

**Tactics:**
The goblins operate in complete darkness. They can see perfectly (darkvision, Grit-Snuff applied). They wait for the players to fumble, then attack from multiple angles simultaneously — using the noise of the screens to cover their movement.

*   **Players have no light and no darkvision:** Goblins attack with **Advantage** on every roll. Player attacks are at **Disadvantage** and cannot target specific enemies without a **DC 15 Perception check** (sound only).
*   **Players use the Grit-Snuff:** Combat becomes standard. The goblins lose their advantage entirely.

**What the players see if light briefly flares:**
Three goblins with dead, black-rimmed eyes crouching among the fallen screens. They do not squint at the light. They stare straight into it, pupils fixed and flat.

**Loot:**
*   **Pouch of Grit-Snuff** on the body of the largest goblin — a leather pouch holding **4 doses** of dry black powder. A thin sheet of bark tucked inside is covered in crude pictograms showing a goblin pressing the powder into its own eyes.
    > *Grit-Snuff:* Grants **Darkvision (30ft) for 10 minutes**. When it wears off, the user takes **1d4 poison damage** as the eyes readjust.

---

### Room 3 — The Gnawed Pantry

*A low-ceilinged storage room. Stolen grain sacks, clay pots of rendered fat, and bundles of something wrapped in burlap line the walls. The smell is rank — rancid fat, wet fur, and something worse underneath. Two crude iron brackets are driven into the wall, each holding a burning torch — the first light the players have seen since the entrance. Two goblins squat near a half-eaten carcass, fighting over a strip of gristle. They are hissing and shoving each other and are paying attention to nothing else.*

**Enemies:** 2 Goblins, 1 Giant Rat.

**The Light:** This room is fully sheltered from the wind. The torches burn steadily. Players can see everything normally. The goblins still have black-painted eyes — they use the powder habitually, because they spend most of their time in the dark tunnels — but in this room the players fight on even terms.

**Tactics:**
The goblins are genuinely distracted — mid-argument, shrill and absorbed. Players can attempt to sneak past or stage an ambush.
*   **DC 12 Stealth** to pass undetected.
*   On a failed check, the goblins react — and the Giant Rat, disturbed, emerges from behind the grain sacks.

**The Powder Clue:**
A short shelf holds four small clay pots. Three contain rendered fat. One contains dry black powder — **1 dose of Grit-Snuff**, with a crude eye symbol scratched into the lid. Next to the shelf, at eye-height, someone has used the black paste to draw a large unblinking eye directly onto the wall. It is the only deliberate decoration in the room. This is how goblins mark where the supply is kept.

**Loot:**
*   **Scavenged Rations:** 3 days' worth of dried, heavily salted meat. Barely palatable.
*   **Clay pot:** 1 dose of Grit-Snuff.

---

### Room 4 — The Foreman's Nook

*A small side chamber, once used as an office or foreman's rest. A collapsed desk, a rusted iron brazier glowing with a low coal fire, a stool. A crude map is scratched into the plaster wall — branching tunnels, a circle that probably means "Krag," and a line leading upward toward the outside.*

**Enemies:** None.

**The Light:** Sheltered and warm — the brazier has been tended recently. A torch brought in from the tunnels will stay lit here. The players can catch their breath, tend wounds, and think.

**The Map:**
The wall map is crude but legible. It confirms the general layout — including a passage leading upward from the final chamber. **DC 10 Investigation** to interpret it correctly. The map makes clear: the exit is on the far side of Krag's room.

**Key Find:**
Tucked under the collapsed desk, in a rotted leather satchel, is a **goblin medicine kit**: three corked vials of dark oily liquid, a small grinding stone stained black, and a folded cloth still caked with dried black paste.
*   The grinding stone makes it clear: the Grit-Snuff is made here.
*   **DC 10 Arcana or Nature:** The dried paste on the cloth shows faint mineral shimmer. When reconstituted with moisture (saliva works), it can still be applied to the eyes. **2 additional doses of Grit-Snuff** are recoverable from the cloth.

**Secondary Loot:**
*   A small metal token, tarnished brass, stamped with a **sun-in-chains symbol** — not goblin-made. This is Mitraic devotional coinage. It was taken from someone.

---

### Room 5 — The Guard Post

*The tunnel narrows sharply before opening into a wider room. Someone has built a barricade across the chokepoint: two overturned mine carts stacked awkwardly, sharpened wooden stakes driven into the gaps. It looks crude. It works.*

**Enemies:** 2 Goblin Sentries.

**The Dark:** No wind reaches this room — a player could light a torch here and it would stay lit. But doing so is a trap: the sentries see any light source from 60ft away, and the tunnel leading here is straight. The sentries have deliberately removed every torch from this room. It is kept dark on purpose.

**Tactics:**
The sentries sit behind the barricade in the dark. They can see everything. They cannot hear players approaching if players move carefully (**DC 13 Stealth**). But if players carry any light, the sentries see it from 60ft away and immediately ready their crossbows.

*   **Players approach in the dark (with Grit-Snuff active):** They can see the sentries before being seen. This enables a proper ambush — or an attempt to talk.
*   **Players approach with light:** The sentries open fire immediately — two bolts — then retreat to warn Krag. Players have **1 round** before Krag is alerted.
*   **Forcing the barricade:** DC 16 Athletics. Loud. Players have **1 round** before Krag is alerted.

**Loot (on the sentries):**
*   **The Jagged Shank:** A crude dagger, its edge serrated from being ground against stone. Deals an extra **1d4 bleed damage on a critical hit**.
*   One sentry carries **2 doses of Grit-Snuff** in a small horn container — clearly standard-issue sentry supply.

---

### Room 6 — The Scavenger's Throne

*The mine opens into its largest chamber, the ceiling vaulting upward into shadows. Three iron brackets on the walls hold burning torches — more light than anywhere else in the mine. The room almost looks like a hall. In the centre, built on a raised platform of stacked stone slabs, sits a throne of rusted iron gears, cracked mine wheels, and the long bones of something large. On it sits Krag.*

**Enemies:** Goblin Chief (Krag), 2 Goblins.

**The Light:** The throne room is torchlit when the players arrive. Krag likes to be seen. This changes on Round 1 of combat.

**Krag:**
He is larger than the others, his scrap-iron breastplate hammered from three mismatched plates of different metals. His eyes are ringed with so much Grit-Snuff paste that in torchlight they look like black holes in his face. He does not blink. He watches the players enter.

He speaks a few words of Common — badly — and will attempt to negotiate if the players survive his opening gambit. He is territorial but not suicidal. His terms: leave the mine, leave the ridge. His offer: safe passage out — he will call off his remaining goblins and let the players walk back through unmolested.

**Opening Gambit (Round 1 of combat):**
Krag opens by hurling a prepared sack of loose mineral dust — a flash of choking powder that simultaneously blinds and kills every torch in the room. The chamber goes pitch black. **DC 12 Constitution save** or be **Blinded for 1 round**. He then has his 2 goblins flank while the players are disoriented. If the players have the Grit-Snuff active, they can see in the sudden dark. If they don't, they are fighting blind.

**Combat Tactics:**
*   Krag uses **Leadership** — goblins within 10ft have **Advantage on their first attack roll**.
*   **Heavy Pick:** +5 to hit, 1d8+3 piercing.
*   If reduced to half HP, Krag retreats behind the throne and attempts to use the dark, calling the goblins to screen him.

**Loot:**
*   **Krag's "Crown":** A dented silver bowl stolen from a Mitraic pilgrim. Worth **25gp**. Any follower of Mitra who sees it worn will recognise it — and the wearer as a thief.
*   **Pilgrim's Purse (on Krag's belt):** A leather coin purse, not goblin-made — too well-stitched, too clean. Inside: **15gp** in mixed coin and a small rolled scrap of parchment. The parchment shows a rough hand-drawn sketch of a road descending from a ridge, with a circle at the bottom labelled *"The Village"* in shaky Common script. Someone was heading there. They didn't make it.

---

### The Way Out

The players must go back the way they came. The mine's passages do not change, but navigating them in the dark — with any remaining goblins now aware something is wrong — is a different proposition on the return trip.

The wind is a partial guide: it flows steadily from the deeper tunnels toward the entrance, so following it will always trend the players in the right direction. But the branching passages in Room 1 are still a problem — the wind splits equally between them, and two of the three exits are wrong. **Without darkvision**, the players are back to guessing. **With the Grit-Snuff active**, the worn floor of the correct tunnel is visible, same as on the way in.

This is the last use of the powder. Players who rationed it will have enough. Players who burned through it in combat will be navigating the final stretch by touch and luck.

Once through Room 1, the entrance shaft is straight ahead — the cold outside air hits them before they see the light.

## VI. THE RIDGE

The players crawl out of the entrance shaft one at a time, back into the open air. The sky is grey-white and featureless. They are standing on the same ridge they descended from. But now, looking down for the first time from this angle, they can see it below them: a valley shrouded in a wall of fog that sits absolutely still — not drifting, not shifting. The Silent Village is in there somewhere.

The fog looks wrong. It looks like a boundary.

*(Simulation hint: the same wind that raked through the mine with enough force to snuff a torch does not disturb the fog in the valley below. Not one tendril moves.)*
